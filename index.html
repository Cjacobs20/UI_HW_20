A team oriented project with the goal of producing and designing a mobile application to help our users to be successful in all things plant ownership related

The Problem:

Most people love what plants do to their living spaces and their life, but as we add more plants, complexity scales, and before long there’s too many tasks for a person to keep track of, causing the plants they love to become a burden.

The Solution:

Create a simple, fun, powerful app that can easily track your plant tasks, as well help you diagnose and treat plant problems. With A.I. plant and room scanning, and automatic plant care scheduling, Homegrown makes it easy to keep track of all your plants in one place. And to help with motivation, Care Challenges, Reward Badges and Achievement Streaks will make watering fun!

Our Project at a Glance:

The goal of this project was to define keys features which our user, Janet Buck, would benefit from the most in a plant care application, in order to create the most market competitive app we could. With Figma, PhotoShop, trello, Instagram and Google Drive at our disposal, we had three weeks to create a fully clickable prototype.

User Research:

Based on a proto-persona created to meet our likely demographic, a user interview plan was created then conducted with 5 seperate users. As user researchers, we wanted to 1.) understand why a person might feel overwhelmed and/or forgetful about plant care, 2.) understand what they wish they could have learned (or still remembered) about the plants they own, and 3.) understand what works and what doesn’t work to help users find motivation to complete tasks. An auxilliary Instagram survey was aslo conducted to cast an even wider net to gauge our demographich. Key findings were synthesized into an affinity diagram, which aided in a sharper image of our user, funnelling into a full user persona.

Ideation:

With a clearer idea of theapplication we aimed to build in mind, we then outlined a story board of how a user discovering our service might look. A user flow was then created to outline how the basic flow through Homegrown should go, which would then be used as the blueprint for our initial wireframing.

Prototyping:

Lofi wireframes were first quickly created by each individual on my team, to gather some base, unbiased impressions of each designers thinking. These individual renderings were then compared, contrasted and discussed, to identify the pieces we wanted to keep and glue together. Once this was decided, the amalgum was formed into a sigle, mid-fidelty prototype for usability testing to be performed on. After usability was completed, key learings were identified and implemented into a "final" ( a designers work is never done) prototype.

Testing:

Testing provided many insights to be implemented in our configuration. some of these insights included; application of side scrolling to free up space, changing color consistencies, expanding the loading screen during the plant ID function to help the user understand what was occurring, to name the major ones

Conclusion:

While our UX process proved highly fruitful for understanding our user needs and testing affirmed those needs and fixed others, the final product could have been better fleshed out given more time. We conducted a rather extensive competitor analysis, which could have bread more handsome visual design of our product. That aside, the functionallity fully executed the tasks me and my team identified to be most important to the user, and therefore, I believe this product was ultimately a success.
